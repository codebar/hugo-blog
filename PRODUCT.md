# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Potential students** discovering codebar and considering joining a workshop
- **Current community members** (students, coaches, organizers) staying connected with stories and events
- **Tech industry folks and potential sponsors** learning about codebar's impact and values

All audiences share a common goal: understanding what codebar is, what it stands for, and how they can be part of it.

## Product Purpose

The codebar blog exists to share authentic stories from our community — event recaps, coach and student journeys, and reflections on diversity in tech. It documents the human side of learning to code and the connections formed through workshops. Success means readers feel inspired to participate (as students, coaches, or sponsors) and connected to a welcoming, grassroots community making tech more accessible.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, welcoming, community-driven, authentic, grassroots.**

The tone is conversational and inclusive — like a friendly coach explaining something over coffee, not a corporation making an announcement. We celebrate individual stories and small victories. The design should feel approachable and human, not polished to the point of feeling distant.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate sterile**: Avoid overly polished, bland, "professional" aesthetics that feel like enterprise SaaS marketing
- **Trendy/over-designed**: Avoid design fads, excessive gradients, glassmorphism, or whatever is currently popular on Dribbble
- **Generic tech blog**: Avoid Medium-style minimalism or Substack sameness

## Design Principles

1. **Community-first**: The content and people are the heroes; design supports without competing
2. **Authentic storytelling**: Layout and typography should feel like a well-crafted zine or community newsletter, not a magazine
3. **Approachable warmth**: Colors and spacing should invite reading, not impress with technique
4. **Accessible by default**: Inclusive design is core to codebar's mission; WCAG AA minimum
5. **Timeless over trendy**: Favor enduring design decisions over current fashions

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Readable type at comfortable sizes (18px+ body minimum)
- Sufficient color contrast for all text
- Respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Keyboard-navigable throughout
